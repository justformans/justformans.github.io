Products that help with arthritis

Products that help with arthritis

Arthritis is a chronic disease characterized by inflammation and joint pain. Although there is no curative diet that saves from arthritis, scientists have found that certain foods reduce it symptoms and increase the overall immune system.

Below are the products that are suitable for fighting arthritis.

Fish.

Fatty fish, such as salmon, are a very good source of omega-3 fatty acids (O-3A). O-3 LCAS help control the inflammatory process characteristic of arthritis and alleviate symptoms. Try to include fish in your menu at least once a week. If you are not a fan of this food, talk to your doctor about supplements containing o-3C.

Broccoli and Brussels sprouts.

These vegetables contain the nutrient sulforaphane, which protects the cartilage in the joints from damage due to osteoarthritis's. In addition, these vegetables are generally good for health, and experts recommend including them in the menu as often as possible.

Garlic.

Garlic belongs to the group of bulbous plants along with onions and leeks. These plants contain the ingredient diallyl disulfide, which helps in the treatment of a number of diseases, including arthritis.

Cherry.

It has been proven that cherries reduce the incidence of gout. Scientists believe that anthocyanins contained in the fruits of this plant have anti-inflammatory properties and contribute to the relief of symptoms in people suffering from both gout and arthritis. Anthocyanins are also found in other red fruits, such as strawberries and raspberries.

Dairy produce.

Milk and other dairy products are some of the best sources of calcium and vitamin D, which are important for bone and joint health. Vitamin D supports the absorption of calcium from bones and has been proven to, helps strengthen the immune system. In addition to dairy products, calcium and vitamin D are found in large quantities in green leafy vegetables.

Green tea.

It contains a lot of antioxidants and polyphenols that relieve inflammation and slow down damage to tendons and joints. In addition, the researchers it is said that green tea includes a specific antioxidant-epigallocatechin-3-gallate, which suppresses the synthesis of molecules that provoke joint destruction.

Citrus.

Citrus fruits, such as orange and grapefruit, are among the best sources of vitamin C. Studies show that daily intake of the right amount of this vitamin helps maintain joint health and protects against osteoarthritis.
