Foods to avoid for ulcerative colitis

Foods to avoid for ulcerative colitis

Ulcerative colitis (UC) is an inflammatory bowel disease that causes inflammation and ulceration of the lining of the colon and rectum. The disease is chronic character and has a recurrent course with periodic exacerbations, in which patients complain of bloating, intestinal pain, diarrhea, accompanied by blood impurities.

The exact cause of YAK has not yet been fully clarified. Scientists believe that this disease may be autoimmune, but so far no the final opinion on this issue. Some foods and eating habits can worsen the condition of a UC patient.

Whole grains.

Food with a high fiber content is often difficult to digest in UC, and its consumption can cause unpleasant symptoms. Experts advise to avoid products made from unseeded flour, such as pasta, bread, pasta products, cereals.

Dairy produce.

Very often, people with UC do not tolerate the use of dairy products. If you notice that this type of food causes you to complain, remove it from your diet, including butter, yogurt, and cheese. To to exclude these foods from your menu, you can follow an exclusionary diet, which is compiled in conjunction with a gastroenterologist.

Legume products.

Legumes are rich in fiber and cause increased gas formation, which makes them unsuitable food for people suffering from UC. But they are very an important part of our diet, especially for vegetarians and vegans. If you do not want to give up legumes, try eating them in smaller quantities; you can also try to remove the outer shell after soaking, to later facilitate their digestion.

Coffee and tea.

You know that coffee and black tea increases diuresis and dehydration, and in patients with UC, this can cause some unpleasant symptoms. In addition, coffee and black tea provoke diarrhea, which is especially undesirable in this disease.

Vegetables with a high fiber content.

This group includes broccoli, garden cabbage, Brussels sprouts and celery. Their digestion takes longer in the stomach and intestines, which leads to increased flatulence and bloating.

You can tolerate these vegetables more easily if they are cooked well and cut into small pieces. If they still affect you badly, you can replace them other vegetables such as carrots, cauliflower, sweet potatoes.

Corn and mushrooms.

Corn and mushrooms are poorly digested. Very often they remain undigested after the digestive process, which can cause intestinal irritation and diarrhea - all complaints that are undesirable in people with UC. Experts recommend that patients avoid using these products during the period of exacerbation of the disease, so as not to worsen the condition.

Chocolate.

The use of chocolate in patients with UC can lead to spasms and increased defecation, especially in exacerbations of the disease. Of course, this does not mean that you should completely abandon your favorite chocolate. You need to learn to be careful with the amount and eat less of it, so as not to worsen the disease.
