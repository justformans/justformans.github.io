Nutritional deficiencies that can cause depression

If you feel a recent decline in strength, it may be due to a slight cold or infection. But your malaise may also be caused by a lack of some important nutrients nutrient. When there are not enough nutrients in the body, fatigue and depression are felt.

Currently, depression is a very common mental disorder for which doctors most often prescribe antidepressants. However, they rarely refer patients for tests, to determine if they have metabolic problems or if their body lacks important nutrients. There is a clear link between depression and nutritional deficiencies. Here are some of the most common nutritional deficiencies that can cause depression.

Omega-3 fatty acids acids (O-3 LC).

You've probably heard about these fatty acids and their importance to the human body. O-3 LCDS help reduce bad cholesterol and improve overall health. In addition, O-3 LCDS support brain activity, including memory and mood. The researchers found that patients with depression often have low levels Of o-3 LCD.

To get enough O-3A, eat more Flaxseed, fish oil, and walnuts. These fatty acids are found in large quantities in some fish, such as salmon, tuna, and flounder.

Vitamin D.

Doctors often they warn us that vitamin d deficiency can occur in winter when there is no sun, because its synthesis in our body requires sunlight. In winter, we often stay at home longer, and this can lead to vitamin D deficiency, resulting in depression. Elderly people and people who have been working in the company for a long time in offices, most often suffer from a deficiency of this vitamin.

To prevent scarcity, take a walk even in the winter months, when the weather is Sunny. However, if there is a reason why you can not go outside during the day, you can take vitamin D in the form of a dietary Supplement that is prescribed doctor.

B vitamins.

Neuropsychiatrists have shown that There is a link between b vitamins and mood swings, including depression. These vitamins contribute to the production of neurotransmitters in the brain, which are responsible for the connections between different parts of the brain. B vitamins they are found in large quantities in whole grains, nuts, seeds, and dark green vegetables.

Zinc.

Zinc is an indispensable helper in our body. It is involved in more than 250 biochemical processes, including those responsible for mood. In addition, zinc contributes to good digestion. A healthy digestive system is closely linked to good mental health, as the small intestine synthesizes most of the happiness hormone serotonin. Zinc is found in significant amounts in oysters, crabs, chicken, and cashews.

Magnesium.

Like zinc, magnesium is necessary for our body in a number of ways it is also used in the treatment of various biochemical reactions, mainly related to the health of our bones and teeth, reducing anxiety and blood pressure. Magnesium is involved in the activation of enzymes necessary for the synthesis of the hormone serotonin. Good sources of this trace element are nuts and seeds, dark green leafy vegetables, whole grains, and dark chocolate chocolate.
